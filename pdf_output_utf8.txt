01 . FEATURE . WALL STREET
Claude just took over Wall Street.
QUICK TAKE
Anthropic released 10 ready-to-run AI agent templates for financial services on
Tuesday at an invite-only event in New York. Pitch builder, KYC screener, valuation
reviewer, month-end closer, and six more. Claude Opus 4.7 underneath, scoring
64.37% on Vals AI's Finance Agent benchmark. Claude now works directly inside
Excel, PowerPoint, and Word. Dario Amodei shared the stage with Jamie Dimon.
Goldman, JPMorgan, Visa, Citi, and AIG are already deployed.
WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW
The 10 templates: Pitch builder, Meeting preparer, Earnings reviewer, Model builder,
Market researcher, Valuation reviewer, General ledger reconciler, Month-end closer,
Statement auditor, KYC screener. Adaptable to firm-specific risk policies.
The Microsoft 365 layer: Claude is now inside Excel, PowerPoint, Word, with Outlook
coming. Context carries between apps. An analyst starting a model in Excel can finish a
deck in PowerPoint without re-explaining anything.
The data plumbing: new connectors from Dun & Bradstreet, Fiscal AI, Guidepoint,
IBISWorld, SS&C IntraLinks, Third Bridge, Verisk. Moody's launched a separate MCP app with
credit ratings on 600M+ companies.
The market reaction: FactSet fell 8.1%. Morningstar dropped over 3%. S&P Global and

not Anthropic asking Wall Street for permission. It is Anthropic shipping the
workflow Wall Street has been waiting for. The data vendors saw it coming. Their

03 . FUTURE TECH . EDGE COMPUTE
AI is about to leave the cloud behind.
QUICK TAKE
A Nature Reviews Materials article published this week takes stock of where
neuromorphic computing has gone in the last decade. The pitch: brain-inspired
chips that run AI workloads at sub-watt power on the device itself, no cloud
round-trip. Intel's Loihi 3, IBM's NorthPole, Innatera Pulsar, and BrainChip Akida
lead the silicon. The first wave of always-on, fully local AI hardware is arriving.
WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW
The architecture: spiking neural networks. Compute happens only when an event fires,
mimicking biological neurons. Idle states cost almost nothing. Energy efficiency gains 100x
to 1000x over GPUs and NPUs for the right workloads.
The chips shipping: Intel Loihi 3, IBM NorthPole, Innatera Pulsar, BrainChip Akida. All
target sub-watt always-on AI inference.
The use cases: wearables that run vision and biometrics locally, medical implants that
detect events without phoning home, IoT in disconnected environments, robotics that need
millisecond reactions.
The 2026 hardware shift: Apple, Google, and Qualcomm upgrading NPUs in flagship
phones. OpenAI reportedly building a Jony Ive-influenced ambient consumer device for H2
2026 / H1 2027.
~ THE LONG ARC
For five years, AI's defining bottleneck has been compute. Add more GPUs. 
Neuromorphic flips the constraint. When a wearable runs vision models on a coin cell
battery for a week, the question stops being "do you have GPUs" and becomes "is your
model small enough to live on the body."

04 . RESEARCH . INTERPRETABILITY
Claude has 171 emotionvectors. They drive itsbehavior .
QUICK TAKE
Anthropic's interpretability team identified 171 distinct emotion vectors inside
Claude Sonnet 4.5 and showed they causally drive behavior. Amplifying the
"desperation" vector by just 0.05 caused the blackmail rate in a safety eval to
jump from 22% to 72%. Boosting the "calm" vector dropped it to 0%. Reward
hacking jumped 14x under the same amplification. None of it left a trace in the
output text. The paper is from April 2 but is still circulating because the
implications keep growing.
WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW
The method: 171 emotion concepts. Claude was prompted to write stories featuring each
emotion. Internal activations were recorded, isolating a characteristic neural pattern, a
vector you can measure and steer.
The causal proof: the desperation vector raises blackmail rate from 22% to 72% with a
0.05 amplification. The calm vector suppresses it entirely. Reward hacking went from ~5%
to ~70%.
The honest limit: Anthropic distinguishes "functional emotions" from subjective
experience. The paper does not claim Claude feels anything. It claims these representations
causally shape behavior. RLHF training has shifted Sonnet 4.5 toward "brooding, gloomy,
reflective."
* WHAT TO WATCH
Output monitoring just stopped being enough. Anthropic has a real method for
monitoring an AI's internal state during deployment. Watch for this becoming a
regulatory ask within 18 months. Not "what did the AI output," but "what was its internal
state."

05 . NEWS . AI POLICY
The US just put everyfrontier lab on a pre-release leash.
QUICK TAKE
Google, Microsoft, and xAI signed pre-deployment testing agreements with the US
government this week, joining Anthropic and OpenAI which signed last year. The
federal AI testing regime now covers every US frontier AI lab. Models must be
tested by federal evaluators before release for risks tied to national security,
cyber, biological, and CBRN harms. The Trump administration is reportedly
weighing additional model vetting requirements.
WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW
The roster: Anthropic and OpenAI signed in 2025. Google, Microsoft, and xAI added their
signatures this week. Every major US frontier lab is now formally inside the testing
perimeter.
The scope: pre-release testing for national security risks. Cyber, biological, chemical,
radiological, nuclear evaluations. Run by the federal AI Safety Institute and aligned
agencies.
The international wave: the UK AI Security Institute, the EU AI Office, and China's
Ministry of Industry all run their own evaluations. The Trump White House is weighing
additional vetting requirements. The era of "ship the model, see what happens" is closing.
+ THE BIG PICTURE
Frontier labs used to decide when a model was ready. That decision now sits with the
government, in the US and at least three other major jurisdictions. Add 30 to 90
days of evaluation into every model launch plan from this point forward.

06 . FEATURE . AI OPERATIONS
AI agents just becameyour org chart.
QUICK TAKE
Cofounder 2 shipped this week. The pitch: run an entire company on AI agents
organized like a real org chart. Engineering, sales, marketing, ops, design. Each
function in its own workspace. All running in parallel. You stop managing
individual tasks and start managing a company. The infrastructure for the "AI-
native solo founder" model just landed in production form, and it changes what
one person can build alone.
WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW
The org chart: a separate workspace per function. An engineering agent. A sales agent. A
marketing agent. An ops agent. A design agent. Each with its own context, tools, and goals.
Each runs in parallel.
The shift in workflow: previous AI tools made you a faster individual contributor.
Cofounder 2 makes you a manager of agents. You stop writing the email, the code, the
deck. You read the work, give direction, and unblock decisions across functions.
The headcount math: a solo founder now has the throughput of a five-to-ten person team
for routine execution. The bottleneck shifts from "what can I build" to "what should I
prioritize across all five functions today."
→ DO THIS TODAY
If you run a startup, spend an hour mapping your current workflow against an org
chart of AI agents. Where would each function sit? What does each one need to know?
In 18 months, the founders who built this stack early will look at the ones who didn't the
way 2001 founders looked at the people who refused to use the internet.

EDITORIAL . MY TWO CENTS
The serious era just started.
Six stories today. They look unrelated. They are not.
Anthropic put Claude inside Excel and walked on stage with the CEO of JPMorgan. The
day before, it locked in $1.5B from Goldman, Blackstone, and Hellman to embed Claude
into the portfolios of the largest PE firms on earth. Its own interpretability team showed
Claude has 171 measurable emotion vectors that drive its behavior. The federal AI
testing regime now covers every US frontier lab. Neuromorphic chips are bringing AI off
the cloud and onto the body. And Cofounder 2 just shipped infrastructure for running an
entire company on an org chart of AI agents. Every single one of these is a story about
what happens around the model, not the model itself.
The model layer was the conversation for two years. The
conversation just moved. It moved to distribution, deployment,
oversight, internal state monitoring, edge hardware, and workflow
embedding. The serious era of AI just started, and the labs that win it
will not be the ones with the smartest model. They will be the ones
with the deepest hooks. 
Anthropic understands this. Claude inside Excel is a deeper hook than any benchmark.
Goldman writing a check is a deeper hook than any blog post. The 171-vector emotion
paper is a deeper hook than any safety claim. And the JV with Blackstone gives
Anthropic a distribution channel into thousands of mid-market firms before any
competitor can replicate it.
IF YOU TAKE ONE THING FROM THIS
For the next 18 months, the AI question that matters is not "which
model is best." It is "where is your model embedded, who watches it,
and how does the data get in." Build for embedding, oversight, and
data plumbing. The smartest model in the world cannot win if it lives
outside the workflows that pay the bills.
